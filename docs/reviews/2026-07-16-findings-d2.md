# Findings — D2: DM humanness / dynamism (Session D, 2026-07-16)

Reviewer: Fable 5. Scope: does the DM behave like a real person running a table —
steering, adaptation, reincorporation, pacing, rote-tell resistance, consequence, memory,
voice, fairness, surprise. Primary artifact: `skills/dnd/SKILL.md` (the behavior
contract), plus the table-facing procedures in `SKILL-commands.md` and the templates the
prose executes against. Includes the bounded DM-craft research pass the brief called for
(sources at bottom; translated into concrete prose rewrites, not a reading list).

Per ground rules: **propose-only.** Every fix below is a candidate for the Session F
batch wave; nothing was edited. Line numbers are 1-based (`grep -n`-verified).

Verdict up front: the prose is **unusually strong on rote-tell suppression and narration
discipline** — the guards are concrete, mechanical, and mostly airtight (see "What works"
at the bottom). The dynamism failures are almost all of one shape: **a mechanism was
designed, and the wiring that fires it at the table was never written.** The flagship
authored path is the biggest victim — three of its core dynamism systems (steering,
pre-emption revision, prep consumption) are dead on a literal reading of the prose.

## HIGH

### D2-1. `type: authored` matches no steering gate, no end-of-session arc check, and no revision path — ADR-0003's reuse decision was never written into the table prose

The two steering sections in SKILL.md gate literally:

- SKILL.md:225 — "**Structured campaign arc steering** (when `## Campaign Arc` has `type: structured`)"
- SKILL.md:243 — "**Dynamic campaign arc steering** (when `## Campaign Arc` has `type: dynamic`)"

A prepped campaign is `type: authored` (templates/state.md:155; seeded uncommented at
prep step 6, SKILL-commands.md:335). It matches **neither** gate. The intended behavior —
authored reuses the nine dynamic rules via the state.md mirror — is recorded only in
docs/adr/0003 (never loaded at the table) and a comment in the state template
(templates/state.md:151-154). A model executing SKILL.md as written has **no steering
rules in force** for the flagship path: no world-pressure-before-beat, no
consequences-not-events discipline, no never-show-the-arc rule.

Downstream, the same gate kills the adaptation loop:

- SKILL-commands.md:448 — `/dm:dnd end` step e: "**Arc check** (dynamic arcs only — skip
  for sandbox/structured): If `## Campaign Arc` has `type: dynamic`…". Authored → the
  beats-landed check, the **pre-emption check** (SKILL-commands.md:452-455), and the
  `steering_notes` refresh are all skipped. CONTEXT.md's "Pre-emption … checked at every
  `/dm:dnd end`" is false for authored campaigns.
- SKILL-commands.md:698 — "`advance`/`revise`/`new` are active only when … `type:
  dynamic`". So when players pre-empt an authored beat (the exact scenario SKILL.md:261
  rule 8 architects three landing paths for), **no procedure exists**: `end` never
  detects it, `arc revise` is a no-op, and `beat complete` (SKILL-commands.md:346-363)
  only advances — it cannot reshape a beat. The spine can only ever be played exactly as
  written or silently drift.

One more staleness: the dynamic preamble (SKILL.md:245) says the arc "was auto-generated
at campaign creation from the world's threat, factions, and Three Truths" — wrong for an
authored mirror, whose beats came from `/dm:dnd prep` (compass already part-flagged this).

**Why HIGH for D2:** steering, world pressure, and pre-emption revision *are* the
dynamism machinery — "the world reacts and bends instead of railroading or dissolving."
The flagship path has it all designed and none of it wired.

**Fix (F wave, prose only):**

1. SKILL.md:243 — before: `**Dynamic campaign arc steering** (when `state.md → ##
   Campaign Arc` has `type: dynamic`):`
   after: `**Dynamic campaign arc steering** (when `state.md → ## Campaign Arc` has
   `type: dynamic` **or `type: authored`** — prepped campaigns carry the same beat
   window in state.md and steer by these same rules; the only differences: beat ids are
   ints, completion goes through `/dm:dnd beat complete` instead of `arc advance`, and a
   revision must also be written back to `spine.json`):`
2. SKILL.md:245 — before: "The arc was auto-generated at campaign creation from the
   world's threat, factions, and Three Truths —"
   after: "The arc was generated at campaign creation (`/dm:dnd new`, from the world's
   threat, factions, and Three Truths) or at `/dm:dnd prep` (the spine's beat window) —"
3. SKILL-commands.md:448 — before: "(dynamic arcs only — skip for sandbox/structured):
   If `## Campaign Arc` has `type: dynamic`, do all of:"
   after: "(dynamic **and authored** arcs — skip for sandbox/structured): If `##
   Campaign Arc` has `type: dynamic` or `type: authored`, do all of:" — with step ii
   gaining one clause: "(authored: run `/dm:dnd beat complete <id>` instead of `arc
   advance`)".
4. SKILL-commands.md:698 + the `revise` flow (:723-733): extend `revise` to authored —
   same three landing paths, plus a final step "authored arcs: write the revised
   `what_changes`/`world_pressure` back to the matching beat in `spine.json` so mirror
   and spine stay in step." ⚠ This creates a new spine dual-write — **Session E must
   sanity-check** it against the existing mirror-drift fragile link before F applies it.

### D2-2. The prep bible's creative core is sealed from the table: no step ever reads a beat's `situation`, `threats`, or `secret` while the party is playing that beat

The state.md mirror deliberately carries only `id / act / label / what_changes /
world_pressure / status` (prep step 6, SKILL-commands.md:337-338; templates/state.md:162-168).
The load procedure forbids opening the spine (SKILL-commands.md:103-107: "Do **NOT**
load at session start … Read `spine.json` only when a beat completes"), and `beat
complete` reads it only for `level_up_to` and `gear` (SKILL-commands.md:346-363, steps
3-4) — i.e. **after the beat is over**.

Net effect at the table, for every authored beat:

- `situation` — "what the party walks into" (templates/spine.md:36) — never read.
- `threats` — the CR-banded, dramaturgically-chosen encounter list D1 reviewed the
  quality of — never read. Combat is improvised blind; nothing stops out-of-band or
  off-tone monsters that the schema gate (prep step 3) was built to prevent.
- `secret` (templates/spine.md:42) — never read, so never revealed. The one field that
  exists purely to reward play never enters play.

This compounds D1's findings: even where prep produces good tailored content, the table
never consumes it. The DM runs on `label` + one-line `world_pressure` + whatever
`steering_notes` says — which is exactly the recipe for generic improvised scenes with
the campaign's names pasted on.

**Fix (F wave):** make the *current beat* hot, keep the rest of the spine cold.

1. `beat complete` step 2 (SKILL-commands.md:352-356) — append: "While `spine.json` is
   open, read the **new current beat's** full entry and refresh state.md's mirror for
   the beat now in play: regenerate `steering_notes` to carry (a) the beat's
   `situation` in one sentence, (b) its `threats` list verbatim, (c) its `secret` (or
   'none'). state.md is DM-side and sealed from the host (prep step 7), so this leaks
   nothing."
2. Prep step 6 (SKILL-commands.md:333-339) — the seeded `steering_notes` for beat 1 must
   carry the same three items (today it only says "authored for beat 1" with no content
   contract — SKILL-commands.md:339).
3. Cheaper alternative if the owner dislikes fattening `steering_notes`: permit one
   targeted read of the current beat's spine entry at load step 5 (bounded — a single
   JSON object, not the file's narrative whole). Either way, the rule to state is:
   **"the current beat's spine entry is table-state; the rest of the spine is cold
   storage."**

### D2-3. Character Pillar is planted at creation and never consumed at the table — the personalization loop has no second half (D1 handoff, confirmed)

The sheet template carries `## Character Pillar` with "**Active hooks:** how the DM is
currently using this pillar in play — **update each session**"
(templates/character-sheet.md:8-11). Pillar derivation is important enough to be
Opus-routed (SKILL.md:186). And then: **"pillar" appears nowhere in Active DM Mode or
the 13 standards.** No save/end step touches "Active hooks" (checked: SKILL-commands.md
366-457 in full). The template's "update each session" is an instruction addressed to
nobody — no procedure executes it. The only oblique consumption gestures are the
re-engagement toolkit's backstory tool (SKILL.md:79) and the leading-questions habit
(SKILL.md:127), neither of which mentions the pillar or the sheet section.

This is the Lazy DM's step 1 ("review the characters") existing as data with no read
path — the exact mechanism that makes a table feel *this party's* game instead of a
generic one. D1-C1 found prep ignores the sheets; this finding is its table-time twin.

**Fix (F wave):**

1. Standard 9 (SKILL.md:121-127) — append one paragraph: "**Play the pillars.** At
   session open, re-read each PC's `## Character Pillar`. Before the session's pressure
   point, aim at least one scene, NPC, or complication at a pillar — threaten a Bond,
   tempt a Flaw, dangle a Goal, test an Ideal. When a pillar fires, note it in the
   sheet's `Active hooks` line. If the pillar section is blank, the leading-questions
   habit below is how you fill it — record the answer and derive the pillar then."
2. `/dm:dnd save` (after the Live State Flags step, SKILL-commands.md:371-376) — add:
   "**Pillar hooks:** for each PC whose pillar was touched this session, update the
   `Active hooks` line in their sheet's `## Character Pillar` (one line: how it's
   currently in play)."
3. Standard 6's session shape (SKILL.md:110) — one clause: "…a pressure point roughly
   two-thirds through that forces a meaningful decision or escalation — **preferably one
   aimed at a Character Pillar** —…".

## MEDIUM

### D2-4. "A failed roll complicates" names no source for the complication — failure never advances the world's agenda (PbtA move-menu gap)

SKILL.md:117 handles failure shape well (sideways-not-dead-end, degree-of-failure lever,
the puzzle carve-out). But the complication palette is scene-local: "a partial success
with a cost, the goal at a price, or a fresh problem." Nothing connects failure to the
campaign's *active threats* — which is the Apocalypse World insight the brief asked to
mine: on a miss, the GM makes a move **from the world in motion** ("announce future
badness," "advance a threat," "make your move, but misdirect"), and a player ignoring a
telegraphed danger is a "golden opportunity" that licenses a hard move. Without this,
long-run failures trend toward disposable local color while factions stand still
between saves (see also D2-8).

**Fix (F wave):** append to SKILL.md:117: "Draw the complication from the world in
motion, not from thin air. Prefer, in order: (1) an active faction or NPC visibly
advances their current goal one step; (2) the telegraphed danger gets one step closer —
on-screen, so the player sees it coming; (3) a concrete cost lands — resource, position,
cover, an ally's patience; (4) the situation worsens or splits — reinforcements, an
alarm, a third party arrives. If the player earlier ignored a danger you telegraphed,
failure is when it arrives full-force. Never name the machinery — deliver the
complication as fiction, as if it was always going to happen."

### D2-5. The session shape has no clock — "two-thirds through" is unmeasurable in a chat session

SKILL.md:110 engineers a session as opening → pressure point "roughly two-thirds
through" → closing beat. At a physical table the DM reads a wall clock; in chat nothing
defines session length, and no procedure asks the player or counts anything. So the
shape cannot actually be executed, and the predictable failure is the exact one the
paragraph warns against: the session "simply stops" when the player logs off, no
pressure point, no closing beat.

**Fix (F wave):**

1. Load step 1 (SKILL-commands.md:68-74) already asks one AskUserQuestion — add a second
   option row: *"How long are you playing today?"* → `short / standard / open-ended`,
   stored as `session_length` in `## Session Flags`.
2. SKILL.md:110 — after "roughly two-thirds through", add: "Chat has no wall clock —
   count **scenes**: short session ≈ pressure point by scene 2, standard ≈ scene 3-4;
   `open-ended` → re-raise the pressure roughly every 3-4 scenes instead. At each scene
   boundary (the same trigger as the micro-save) ask yourself: *has this session had its
   pressure point yet?* When the player signals wrapping up ('one more scene', 'I need
   to stop soon'), engineer the closing beat **now** and offer `/dm:dnd end` after it
   lands."

### D2-6. Engagement signals are table-body signals — never translated to the chat medium

Standard 1's toolkit triggers on "energy flagging, player circling without traction"
(SKILL.md:76); Standard 2 reads "leaning in — asking follow-up questions, roleplaying
deeply" (SKILL.md:85). Half of these are physical-table tells a chat DM cannot observe.
The prose never says what flagging energy *looks like in text*, so the toolkit's trigger
condition is under-specified exactly where this DM lives.

**Fix (F wave):** insert after SKILL.md:76's toolkit intro: "In chat, read energy from
the shape of the replies. **Flagging:** replies shrink to bare verbs ('attack', 'ok',
'I keep going'), the player picks your first offered option three times running, asks
'what else is here?', or goes meta ('can we skip ahead'). **Engaged:** in-character
dialogue, unprompted backstory or plans, questions about an NPC's motives. Shrinking
replies for two or three turns = pick a re-engagement tool now."

### D2-7. Nothing re-anchors the *behavior contract* (or `roll_mode`) after compaction — the re-read ladder covers facts only

The compaction ladder (SKILL.md:213-221) is excellent for campaign *facts* — but nothing
re-reads the DM's own *rules of conduct* after compaction. The narration principles,
rote-tell guards, and the `roll_mode: players` hard constraint (SKILL.md:276 — flagged
in-prose as "the #1 thing players notice and dislike") live only in prose that
compaction can flatten; `roll_mode` itself sits in `## Session Flags`, which the ladder
never lists, and is not in Live State Flags either. This answers the compass open
question ("what actually prevents auto-roll regression after compaction?"): **nothing
but luck.** Behavioral drift post-compaction — voice flattening, rote tells creeping
back, auto-rolling — has no anchor, while factual drift has five.

**Fix (F wave):** two cheap lines.

1. Ladder first stop (SKILL.md:214) — append: "…and re-read `state.md → ## Session
   Flags` (`roll_mode`, `tutor_mode`, `autosave`, `chaos_factor`) — flag values are
   never trusted from compacted memory."
2. Micro-save rule (SKILL.md:223) — append: "After any context compaction, before your
   next narration: re-read this file's **Narration principles** and **Dice convention**
   sections (targeted section reads). The behavior contract degrades in summary exactly
   like campaign facts do."
   (Session E should double-check feasibility — SKILL.md is the skill entry and its
   re-read path post-compaction may differ from campaign files.)

### D2-8. The world only moves at `/dm:dnd end` — mid-session, factions are frozen unless the DM spontaneously remembers them

Standard 11 (SKILL.md:135) and the Faction Moves write (SKILL-commands.md:380) are
end-of-session. Bangs consume *logged* moves (SKILL.md:141) — logged last session. The
re-engagement toolkit's "faction makes a visible move" (SKILL.md:78) fires only on
drift. In a long session, nothing prompts off-screen faction motion between the opening
and the save — the AW "think offscreen too" principle has a per-session cadence, not a
per-scene one.

**Fix (F wave):** piggyback the existing scene-boundary rhythm. Micro-save rule
(SKILL.md:223) — add one clause to the flush list: "…and ask once: *did any active
faction take a step just now, off-screen?* If yes, append one line to `## Faction
Moves` immediately and let it surface as a sight or rumour within a scene or two —
don't bank it for the session end." (The end-of-session sweep at SKILL-commands.md:380
then becomes a catch-up pass, not the sole mechanism.)

### D2-9. The oracle toolkit is orphaned — real surprise machinery, zero pointers from Active DM Mode

`oracle.py` ships chaos factor, likelihood-shifted yes/no, Mythic event focus, and a
scene-spark generator, with good usage guidance *in the command doc* ("use when the
fiction poses a question the prep doesn't answer" — SKILL-commands.md:794-815). SKILL.md
never mentions any of it. A DM executing the behavior contract will never roll an
oracle, so every "unexpected" turn is actually the model's habit — the precise failure
mode dice-driven oracles exist to break. This also quietly weakens fairness (Standard
7): an oracle roll is visible impartiality.

**Fix (F wave):** one bullet in the narration principles (after SKILL.md:209): "When
the fiction poses a question your prep doesn't answer — or you catch yourself deciding
by default — roll it: `/dm:dnd oracle ask` (yes/no, chaos-weighted) or `oracle event`
(random focus), then interpret the result against current threads and NPCs. Dice keep
your world honest and your surprises real. Adjust the chaos factor at scene ends
(`--pc-won` / `--pc-lost`)."

## LOW

### D2-10. Two five-step attitude scales that don't match

`/dm:dnd npc attitude` ladder: hostile → unfriendly → neutral → friendly → allied
(SKILL-commands.md:604; templates/npcs.md:14). Faction stances: Allied / Friendly /
Neutral / **Suspicious** / Hostile (templates/state.md:45; SKILL-commands.md:373).
"Unfriendly" vs "Suspicious" — same slot, two words, prime post-compaction conflation
material (same genus as the B-session "states vs stances" find). **Fix:** pick one
five-word scale for both ladders (recommend the faction wording — "Suspicious" is more
playable than "unfriendly"); one-line edits in the three spots. → compass fragile-links.

### D2-11. NPC `Current goal` says "update each session" — no procedure updates it

templates/npcs.md:16: "**Current goal:** what this NPC is actively doing … — update
each session." `/dm:dnd save` updates NPC *dispositions* in Live State Flags
(SKILL-commands.md:374) but never touches npcs.md/npcs-full.md goals. Same
dead-template-instruction pattern as D2-3. Stale goals directly undercut SKILL.md:209
("NPCs … pursue agendas independently") — an NPC can't pursue a goal nobody refreshes.
**Fix:** add to save, next to the disposition step: "for NPCs who acted or were acted
on this session, refresh the `Current goal` line in their npcs-full.md entry."

### D2-12. The prose is solo-frame throughout — no spotlight rule for multi-PC parties

"The player," singular, everywhere (SKILL.md:23, :85, :122). The data model supports
multiple PCs (characters/*.md, party status lines), and one player often runs several.
Nothing says to rotate focus. **Fix (one line, Standard 9):** "With multiple PCs, rotate
the aim: this session's pillar scene or pressure point targets a different PC than last
session's (note the last target in `## DM Style Notes`)."

## What works (so F doesn't fix what isn't broken)

- **Rote-tell guards are concrete and mechanical, not aspirational.** The "what do you
  do?" ban ships its replacement behavior (SKILL.md:202); NPC speech is structurally
  separated by format, not by vibes (SKILL.md:203); the moment cap + heat scaling
  (SKILL.md:95-99) puts a number on over-narration; the read-it-aloud test (SKILL.md:102)
  kills novel-voice; tone saturation (SKILL.md:25) kills everything-ominous at the root.
- **Specifics-over-abstraction (SKILL.md:93)** attacks the single most LLM-shaped
  failure (vague fluff in briefings) and even prescribes the recovery move ("improvise
  the specific, then commit to it as canon").
- **The calibration loop is a real compounding mechanism**, not a gesture: end asks →
  `### DM Calibration` → distilled into `## DM Style Notes` only when a pattern sharpens
  (SKILL-commands.md:445-447) → read-and-internalize at every load (SKILL-commands.md:99).
- **Failure-complicates with the puzzle carve-out (SKILL.md:117)** is a genuinely good
  ruling most human DMs get wrong in one direction or the other.
- **The pre-emption design (SKILL.md:261-265) is sophisticated real-DM craft** — three
  landing paths, auto-triggered, player-invisible. It just doesn't fire on the flagship
  path (D2-1) — fix the wiring, don't touch the design.
- **The inventory/roster ground-truth guard (SKILL.md:208)** is the rare anti-"yes-and"
  rule, and it's placed exactly right.
