# Findings — D1: Prep-phase story tailoring (Session C, 2026-07-16)

*Fable review, Session C. Scope: does `/dm:dnd prep` produce a unique story bound to the
settings chosen at prep time, or generic content with the settings pasted on? Propose-only
per review ground rules — no prose was edited; all fixes batch in Session F.*

**Line-number note:** citations are against the files as of Session C (post-premise-variance
merge, 236 tests green). Session F refreshes all citations after the fix wave.

---

## Verdict in one paragraph

The prep pipeline is strong on **variance** (the premise scaffold genuinely forces
non-generic premises), **tone** (locked first, carried through, saturation-ruled at the
table), and **spoiler discipline** (separate-pass shopping lists, terrain-archetype rule).
It is weak on exactly the axis the review brief asked about first: **the party**. The
command names the imported party sheets as an input and then never reads them — not for
hooks, not for level, not for size, not for class composition. A bible prepped for *this*
party and one prepped for *any* party are currently the same bible. Second-order gap: the
flagship prep path produces **zero NPC entries**, while both the spine schema and the
table-time NPC-voicing gate assume named, fleshed-out NPCs exist. Both are fixable with
prep-procedure prose (plus two small script changes to propose).

## HIGH severity

### C1 — Prep never reads the party sheets it declares as input

**Claim.** `/dm:dnd prep` inputs: "premise …, tone …, difficulty, **and the imported party
sheets**" (SKILL-commands.md:279-281). No step of the procedure (0–7,
SKILL-commands.md:283-344) reads `characters/*.md` or uses any PC fact. Verified consumers
of PC data in the whole prep flow: none. The only party-facing artifact, the **Character
Pillar** — built at `character new` step 2 precisely as a hook anchor ("derive ONE pillar —
Bond, Flaw, Ideal, or Goal", SKILL-commands.md:526-528; stored with an "Active hooks" field,
templates/character-sheet.md:8-13) — is never consumed at prep.

**Corroborating absences:**
- `templates/world.md` has no party-facing section — factions, nodes, quest seeds, mystery
  all party-agnostic (world.md:105-161).
- Quest seeds derive "from threat, factions, mystery, NPC motivations"
  (SKILL-commands.md:38, template world.md:157-161) — PCs not on the source list.
- The spine beat schema has no PC-facing field (templates/spine.md:31-43).
- The 2026-07-14 dry-run probe ran prep "with 2 imported party sheets" and the resulting
  world/spine show no trace of them (docs/probes/2026-07-14-prep-dry-run.md:9, 41-56).
- `gear` entries (spine.md:40) carry no guidance to match the actual party's classes —
  nothing stops a spine granting a greatsword to a party of two casters.

**Why it matters.** This is the difference between "a campaign tailored to the party at
prep time" and "a campaign with the party pasted on at session 1." The table-time
re-engagement toolkit even assumes prep did this work: "A backstory thread surfaces — cut
to a location, person, or object tied directly to the character's history" (SKILL.md:79)
has nothing to cut *to* unless the bible planted it.

**Proposed fix (prose, SKILL-commands.md — insert as new step 0.7 after 0.5):**

> 0.7 **Read the party (before authoring anything).** Read every sheet in
> `~/.claude/dnd/campaigns/<name>/characters/` (and confirm with the host if the dir is
> empty — prep binds the bible to the party, so the party comes first). For each PC,
> extract: level, class/subclass, key equipment, and the `## Character Pillar` (raw
> sentence + derived pillar) and `## Backstory & Notes`. Then bind the bible to them:
> - **World:** at least one faction, node, or mystery element must connect to each PC's
>   pillar or backstory (a place they're from, a person they owe, an order that wants
>   what they have). Log the binding in `world.md → ## Party Hooks` (one line per PC:
>   pillar → which world element carries it).
> - **Spine:** at least one beat's `situation` or `secret` must engage each PC's pillar;
>   `gear` rewards must be usable by the actual party's classes.
> - **Quest seeds:** derive at least one seed per PC from their pillar, alongside the
>   threat/faction/mystery seeds.
> If a PC has no pillar (player skipped), bind to class/background instead — never invent
> a pillar (matches `character new` step 2).

Companion template change: add a `## Party Hooks` section to `templates/world.md` (after
`## Quest Seed Bank`) so the binding has a durable, checkable home. This also gives Session
F's fix wave something a future probe can audit: empty `## Party Hooks` = prep skipped the
party.

### C2 — Spine validation hardcodes a level-1 start; real party level and size are ignored

**Claim.** `schema.party_levels()` starts every campaign at level 1
(scripts/prep/schema.py:27, `current = 1`); `templates/spine.md:48-49` documents "The party
starts at level 1"; prep step 2 assumes "arc ends ≈ L8" (SKILL-commands.md:310). But
`/dm:dnd character import` explicitly imports characters *at their current level*
("Import at current level [X], or level up before starting?", SKILL-commands.md:553), and
prep's own input line says the sheets carry level (review brief; sheets:
templates/character-sheet.md:5).

**Failure mode.** Host imports a level-3 party and runs prep: every beat's threat band is
computed as if the party were L1 at beat 1 — beat-1 encounters validate against CR ceiling 3
instead of 5 (bestiary.py:15-24), `level_up_to: 2` passes the schema (schema.py:63-65) while
being nonsense for an L3 party, and the whole "arc ends leveled" guarantee silently
mistunes. Nothing warns — the spine validates green.

**Also missing entirely: party size.** No prep step, schema field, or bestiary flag knows
how many PCs exist. CR banding is per-single-monster vs level (bestiary.py:27-29) — a lone
CR-3 monster at the L1 band ceiling is a very different fight for two PCs than for five,
and 5e's DMG encounter math is party-size-scaled. See also C5 (no counts).

**Proposed fixes:**
1. *Prose (cheap, catches the common case):* in the new step 0.7 (C1), after reading
   sheets: "If any PC is above level 1, stop and tell the host: the spine's leveling and
   threat bands assume a level-1 start. Either level-adjust at import or accept a re-banded
   spine (see below)."
2. *Script (proposal for owner approval):* add `--start-level N` to `schema.py` (threaded
   into `party_levels`) and document `bestiary.py --level` as "party level *during the
   beat*, from the actual starting level." Small, test-covered change; keeps the fixture
   example valid (default 1).
3. **Party block — REQUIRED, owner decision 2026-07-16 (Session C review):** a `party`
   block in `spine.json` (`size`, `start_level`), populated at prep step 0.7 from the
   actual sheets and validated against them. Party size is crucial to bible authoring, not
   just banding: encounter shape (solo vs mob action economy, see C5), quest/seed design
   (a duo gets infiltration and leverage seeds; a five-PC party carries siege-shaped
   situations), and gear distribution all scale with it. Prep step 2's authoring guidance
   must reference `party.size` explicitly when choosing threats and shaping situations.
   This is not F backlog — it ships in the Session F fix wave with C1/C2.

### C3 — The flagship prep path produces a bible with zero NPCs

**Claim.** Prep step 5: "Copy `npcs.md` and `session-log.md` from templates … **(empty)**"
(SKILL-commands.md:327-328). No prep step generates NPC entries, and `npcs-full.md` is
never created (its writers are `new` step 11, SKILL-commands.md:37, and `import` step 5,
SKILL-commands.md:234). Meanwhile:

- The spine's `world_pressure` is by definition "the faction/**NPC** move that makes this
  beat feel inevitable" (templates/spine.md:38) — pressure needs named actors.
- `templates/world.md:166` ends with "*World-gen NPCs: <Name>, <Name>, <Name>*" — a model
  filling the template names NPCs whose entries then exist nowhere.
- The table-time gate "**Before writing substantive dialogue or decisions for any named
  NPC**, read their full entry in `npcs-full.md`" (SKILL.md:212, SKILL-commands.md:109)
  finds nothing to read for an authored campaign's whole first stretch — every bible NPC
  gets improvised from a one-line world.md mention, then back-filled, which is exactly the
  drift the gate exists to prevent.
- Legacy `/dm:dnd new` — the deprecated path — produces **3 full NPCs with a relationship
  web** (SKILL-commands.md:37). The flagship path produces none. Inverted quality.

**Proposed fix (prose, SKILL-commands.md — insert as step 1.5, and amend step 5):**

> 1.5 **NPC layer.** For every NPC named anywhere in `world.md` (factions' leaders, the
> mystery's actors, node stakeholders) — minimum 3 — write a full entry to `npcs-full.md`
> (same fields as `/dm:dnd new` step 11: role, stats, demeanor, motivation, secret, speech
> quirk, faction, current goal, schedule, personality axes, ≥2 relationships) and an index
> row to `npcs.md`. Run the name-registry uniqueness check on each (as `new` does). Every
> spine `world_pressure` that names an actor must name one of these NPCs or factions.

> Step 5 (amended): "Copy `session-log.md` from templates (empty). (`npcs.md` /
> `npcs-full.md` were written at step 1.5.)"

## MEDIUM severity

### C4 — The `difficulty:` argument is a dead input

**Claim.** `/dm:dnd prep [… difficulty:easy|standard|deadly]` (SKILL-commands.md:277) is
named as an input ("tone …, difficulty, and the imported party sheets",
SKILL-commands.md:280) and then consumed by nothing: no prep step mentions it, bestiary.py
has no difficulty parameter (bestiary.py:55-62), and the band math has no difficulty knob
(bestiary.py:15-29). The dry-run probe passed `difficulty:standard` with no observable
effect (docs/probes/2026-07-14-prep-dry-run.md:8). The enum also matches nothing else in
the repo (deprecated xp.py uses easy/medium/hard/deadly).

**Proposed fix — either wire it or cut it (owner's call at F):**
- *Wire:* difficulty shifts the authoring guidance in step 2 — e.g. easy: pick from the
  lower half of the band, solo monsters below ceiling; deadly: ceiling picks and paired
  threats are fair game. One sentence per tier in step 2; optionally a `--difficulty` flag
  on bestiary.py that annotates (not filters) the candidate list.
- *Cut:* remove from the signature at line 277 and the input list at line 280.
  Wiring is the better answer — "how hard should this campaign be" is a real prep-time
  setting the tool currently pretends to accept.

### C5 — Spine threats carry no counts and no encounter budget

**Claim.** `threats` is a flat list of monster names (templates/spine.md:41); there is no
quantity field, no schema rule about total encounter weight, and no authoring guidance
about mob-vs-solo shape (SKILL-commands.md:305-312). "Goblin" could mean one or eight —
at the table the DM improvises the count with zero prep guidance, so the carefully banded
CR math governs only the *species*, not the *encounter*.

**Proposed fix (minimal, prose-first):** allow and encourage `"3x Goblin"` count prefixes
in `threats`; step 2 gains one sentence: "State counts (`3x Goblin`), and shape each fight
deliberately: one at-ceiling solo, a banded pair, or a mob of low-CR minions — the schema
bands the species; you band the action economy." Schema change to parse the `Nx ` prefix is
~10 lines (propose at F; keeps bare names valid so the fixture example still passes).

### C6 — "≥3 hooks (rule of three)" is undefined and homeless

**Claim.** Prep step 2 requires each beat authored with "≥3 hooks (rule of three)"
(SKILL-commands.md:309) "per the schema in `templates/spine.md`" — but spine.md defines no
hooks field, never uses the word "hook," and schema.py validates nothing about hooks. The
term "rule of three" appears exactly once in the repo (that line) with no definition. An
authoring model has no idea where hooks live or what counts as one. (The probe's author
interpreted it as "≥3 ways into each situation," docs/probes/2026-07-14-prep-dry-run.md:55
— reasonable, but that's the probe author guessing, which proves the point.)

**Proposed fix (prose, templates/spine.md — add under the `situation` row or as a note
after the field table):**

> **Hooks (rule of three):** a `situation` must embed at least three genuinely different
> ways in — routes, approaches, or levers a party might use (talk / sneak / fight is the
> degenerate case; better: three different *entry points into the fiction*). Write them
> into the `situation` prose itself; the validator does not check this — it is an
> authoring standard, same register as "situations, not objectives."

And in SKILL-commands.md:309, point at it: "≥3 hooks (rule of three — defined in
templates/spine.md)".

### C7 — The spine and the world layer are not bound to each other

**Claim.** Nothing requires spine content to reference the world.md just authored:
- The dynamic-arc path requires `world_pressure` to "nam[e] actual entities from this
  world" (SKILL-commands.md:49); the prep path's step 2 has no such requirement
  (SKILL-commands.md:305-312), and spine.md:38 only implies it.
- Adventure Nodes (3–5 situations, world.md:133-153) and spine beats (6–8 situations) are
  two parallel situation inventories with no stated relationship. Do beats happen *at*
  nodes? Unstated. Nodes drive Faction Moves when skipped; beats drive steering — if they
  don't share geography and actors, the authored campaign runs on two disconnected maps.
- schema.py cross-validates threats↔bestiary only; no world.md cross-reference exists (fine
  — but then the prose has to carry the requirement, and it doesn't).

**Proposed fix (prose, step 2, two sentences):**

> Every beat's `world_pressure` must name a faction or NPC that exists in `world.md`
> (post-C3: in `npcs-full.md`). Anchor each beat's `situation` at or adjacent to an
> Adventure Node or named world.md location — the spine is the story that moves *through*
> the world layer, not a second world.

### C8 — Shopping lists are prep-frozen; arc revision strands them

**Claim.** `map-list.md` / `ambient-list.md` are generated once at prep step 4 from the
spine's scenes (SKILL-commands.md:315-326). But authored arcs revise at the table
(pre-emption → `arc revise`, SKILL.md:261-264; landing paths can relocate a beat), and the
sound-cue rule is a hard whitelist — "never invent a cue" for anything off the ambient list
(SKILL.md:204). A revised beat that moves the finale from the forge to the flooded docks
plays with no map hint and *no legal ambient cue at all*. No command or prose refreshes the
lists or re-runs `render_assets.py` mid-campaign.

**Proposed fix (prose):** one line in the `arc revise` procedure (SKILL-commands.md:696-733
region): "If the revision relocates or adds a tactical scene or a notable location, append
matching entries to `map-list.md` / `ambient-list.md` (same spoiler rules as prep step 4)
and re-run `render_assets.py` so the host's hub stays current." Cheap, closes the loop.

## LOW severity

### C9 — Bestiary candidates are tone-blind; world knobs unrolled in prep

Two related prep-quality notes, no defect:
- `bestiary.py` filters by CR band only (bestiary.py:45-52); type is printed but nothing
  suggests using it. A horror campaign and a swashbuckling one get the same candidate list;
  tone fidelity of monster choice is pure model judgment over hundreds of rows. *Suggest:*
  one sentence in step 2 — "prefer creatures whose type and flavor fit the tone and
  premise; the band is a legality gate, not a menu order." (Optional later: `--type`
  filter flag.)
- `world.md` Campaign Tone & Genre carries magic level / setting type / danger level
  (world.md:6-8); `/dm:dnd new` rolls or asks for these via the Tone Wizard
  (SKILL-commands.md:26-32) while prep's procedure never mentions them — the model just
  invents values. Consistent with seal-and-walk-away, but worth one line in step 1: "fill
  the remaining Tone & Genre knobs (magic/setting/danger) coherently with the premise
  scaffold's setting axis, and log any you randomize in the d6 format."

### C10 — No post-catalog end-to-end probe exists

The only prep probe (docs/probes/2026-07-14-prep-dry-run.md) predates the tone catalog and
premise scaffold: it used `tone:classic` — not one of the 7 catalog ids (data/tones.yaml:5-25)
— and the now-dead `difficulty:standard` (probe:8). Its findings stand, but nothing has
end-to-end exercised the *current* flow (premise.py scaffold → reconcile → bible). *Suggest
(F backlog):* re-probe after the fix wave; audit specifically for C1's party-binding once
that prose lands.

### C11 — spine.md header calls the seeded block "dynamic arc format"

templates/spine.md:7 — "the spine seeds `state.md → ## Campaign Arc` (**dynamic arc
format**)"; the seeded block is `type: authored` (SKILL-commands.md:333-341,
templates/state.md:151-171). "Dynamic-format window" (the state.md comment's phrasing) is
the defensible term of art per ADR-0003, but the bare "(dynamic arc format)" invites the
exact arc-type confusion CONTEXT.md exists to kill. *Suggest:* "(the authored block —
dynamic-format mirror, see CONTEXT.md 'Arc type')".

## What already works (so the fix wave doesn't regress it)

- **Premise variance is real.** 8×7×7×7 orthogonal axes × 7 tones, rolled not
  free-associated, mandatory reconcile, anti-cliché instruction naming the exact failure
  trope (premise.py:20-25, data/premise-seeds.yaml). Exemplars are quality bars, not
  reusables. This is the strongest part of prep.
- **Tone is locked first and threaded.** Step 0.5 resolves tone before any authoring and
  declares steps 1–6 all author in it (SKILL-commands.md:288-299); table-side saturation
  rule keeps it from flattening every scene (SKILL.md:26). Guard-tested
  (tests/test_prep_skill_prose.py:95-103).
- **Spoiler discipline is enforced by structure.** Separate-pass generation, look-only
  descriptions, terrain-archetype acquire hints (the probe's recommendation was adopted
  into step 4, SKILL-commands.md:315-326).
- **The validation gate is a hard gate and works.** Schema catches band violations, act
  ordering, monotonic leveling, status coherence (schema.py); the probe passed a
  hand-authored real spine first try post-fix.
- **Beats-as-situations discipline** ("situations-not-objectives", consequence-shaped
  `what_changes`) is stated consistently in the dynamic path and inherited by authored.
